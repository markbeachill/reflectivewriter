US4 — Link to Learning Outcomes

Paste the outcome from your rubric and the relevant reflection.

User

Outcome: “demonstrate critical thinking.” My reflection: I thought hard about the project and considered different ideas.

Chatbot

“Thought hard” and “considered different ideas” assert the outcome rather than demonstrating it — a grader can't see the critical thinking yet.

Try this: point to the moment where you actually weighed one idea against another and changed or sharpened your view. Write “I demonstrated critical thinking when I…” and finish it with that specific instance. I'll check it shows the outcome rather than naming it, and that it's pitched at the right level.

The tutor questions, diagnoses and teaches, but never writes your experience, feelings, insight or learning. That boundary is the whole point.

Example safety: Fictional or composite teaching example; no real person, placement, patient, service user, colleague or institution is identifiable.
