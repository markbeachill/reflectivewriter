Mini example

Writer input (pasted into NH4)

On 3 March on Ward 7 at St Elsewhere's, I cared for Mr J, an 82-year-old man with motor neurone disease, who…

Typical tutor response (NH4)

A few things here could identify someone, even though it's just one sentence: the exact date, the ward and hospital name, the patient's initial and age, and a specific condition. Together those could point to one person. Try: “I cared for an older man with a life-limiting neurological condition.” Keep the date and place out. Removing the name alone wouldn't be enough.

What this shows

The check protects real people, and explains why each detail is a risk so the writer learns to anonymise themselves.

This is a short illustration. Real exchanges are interactive: the tutor asks, you answer, and it responds to what you actually wrote.
